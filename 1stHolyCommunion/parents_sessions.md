# Parents' Sessions Journey (First Holy Communion 2025–2026)

These meetings are a six-month journey for parents and carers: not only information, but encounter, conversion, and practical family discipleship.  
The pathway follows the **Five Rs** of the Kerygma:

1. **Relationship** – We were made for communion with God.
2. **Rebellion** – Sin breaks relationship.
3. **Reconciliation** – God rescues us in Jesus.
4. **Re-Creation** – Grace makes us new from within.
5. **Response** – We answer Jesus personally and concretely.

## Session 1 — Relationship (Thu 2 Oct 2025)

**Core theme:** We are loved into existence by the Holy Trinity.

**Main Scriptures used**
- Matthew 3:16–17 (Baptism of Jesus)
- Genesis 1:26–27 (Image of God)
- Ephesians 1:3–5 (chosen in Christ)

**Kerygma focus**
- The Gospel begins with God’s initiative, not our performance.
- Human identity starts with being a beloved son/daughter.
- Introduction to the full Five Rs and Jesus’ question: **“What do you seek?”**

**Key take-home messages**
- Faith starts in relationship, not in rules.
- The Sign of the Cross is a daily identity marker: we belong to God.
- Parents are the first witnesses of the faith at home.

**Key questions we asked**
- What do I most hope for my child this year?
- What has faith looked like in our family so far?
- What does it mean to be made in God’s image?

**Prayers and music used**
- Opening prayer around the candle (Anchor Session 1 opening)
- Silent reflection on Ephesians 1
- Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be
- No formal music focus in this session

**Homework / home conversation with children**
- Bless your child daily with the Sign of the Cross.
- Teach/review a reverent Sign of the Cross before leaving home.
- Tell your child clearly: “You are loved by God.”
- Pray one short family prayer each day (Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be).

## Session 2 — Rebellion (Thu 6 Nov 2025)

**Core theme:** Sin is rupture of relationship, and mercy is always possible.

**Main Scriptures used**
- Genesis 3 (the Fall)
- Luke 22:54–62 (Peter’s denial)
- Luke 6:42 (plank in the eye; catechetical reference)

**Kerygma focus**
- Rebellion is mistrust of the Father’s goodness.
- Sin wounds four relationships: with God, self, others, creation.
- God’s mercy is stronger than shame and despair.

**Key take-home messages**
- Sin is not just “breaking rules”; it is breaking communion.
- The difference between Judas and Peter is hope in mercy.
- The Penitential Rite at Mass is a real return to God.

**Key questions we asked**
- How would I explain sin to my child in simple words?
- When have I felt far from God, and why?
- What is the difference between Judas and Peter?
- What do we mean at Mass by “in my thoughts, words, deeds, and omissions”?

**Prayers and music used**
- Group candle prayer (intercession while passing the candle)
- Glory Be
- Guided Our Father with pauses on forgiveness
- Guardian Angel prayer introduced for home
- No fixed song in this session

**Homework / home conversation with children**
- Practise saying sorry and forgiving quickly at home.
- Introduce a short nightly examen: “Did I love today? Who do I need to forgive?”
- Pray the Guardian Angel prayer at bedtime.
- Keep a brief candle prayer moment once during the week.

## Session 3 — Rescue Begins in History (Thu 4 Dec 2025)

**Core theme:** God sees, hears, and acts; covenant is His loving rescue path.

**Main Scriptures used**
- Exodus 3 (Burning Bush)
- Deuteronomy 6:4–7 (Shema Israel)
- Exodus 20 / Deuteronomy 5 (Ten Commandments in context)

**Kerygma focus**
- God enters ordinary life and calls us by name.
- Covenant is family-bond love, not a cold contract.
- Commandments are words of freedom for a rescued people.

**Key take-home messages**
- Your life story can become “holy ground” where God meets you.
- God’s commandments protect love and freedom.
- We need grace, not willpower alone, to live covenant love.

**Key questions we asked**
- Where do I notice God in daily life right now?
- Who is God for me today?
- Do I see commandments as burdens or guidance from a loving Father?
- What would covenant faithfulness look like in our family this week?

**Prayers and music used**
- Extended Burning Bush prayer with silence
- Song: **“Be Still” (David Kauffman)**
- Shema Israel prayed together
- Optional close: Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be

**Homework / home conversation with children**
- Retell the Burning Bush story in your own words.
- Revisit your “Who is God for me?” card and pray from it.
- Practise the Shema at home.
- At Sunday Mass, point out the three readings and explain their role.
- (Optional) Watch “The Bible in 10 Minutes” (Fr Mike Schmitz) as a family.

## Session 4 — Incarnation: Who Is Jesus? (Thu 8 Jan 2026)

**Core theme:** God does not save from a distance; the Word became flesh.

**Main Scriptures used**
- Luke 1:26–38 (Annunciation)
- John 1:14 (Word became flesh)
- Matthew 2:11 (Magi at the child Jesus)
- Creed line on the Incarnation

**Kerygma focus**
- Rescue reaches its center in the person of Jesus.
- Jesus is not only teacher/prophet; He is Lord.
- Faith requires a personal answer: **“Who do you say I am?”**

**Key take-home messages**
- God waits for a human “yes” (Mary), and invites ours.
- The Incarnation gives dignity and hope to ordinary family life.
- The Creed is a lived pledge, not only words recited.

**Key questions we asked**
- Why did God wait for Mary’s “yes”?
- What holds me back from saying “yes” to God?
- If Jesus is truly God, how does that change prayer and trust?
- Which line of the Creed challenges me most personally?

**Prayers and music used**
- Epiphany responsorial prayer
- Song: **“Here I Am to Worship”**
- Gospel proclamation and closing Epiphany blessing

**Homework / home conversation with children**
- Bring home a Scripture card and place it in a visible place.
- Continue blessing your children regularly.
- Pray together: “Jesus, help us stay close to you.”
- Ask at home: “What gift can we offer Jesus this week (time, forgiveness, service)?”

## Session 5 — Cross, Resurrection, Eucharist (Thu 5 Feb 2026)

**Core theme:** The Cross is personal love; Jesus gives Himself for us and to us.

**Main Scriptures used**
- Romans 8:31–39
- John 15:13
- Philippians 2:5–11
- Matthew 26:26–29 (Last Supper, with children at close)

**Kerygma focus**
- The Cross is not condemnation; it is love to the end.
- Jesus the God-man offers the perfect gift of love for our reconciliation.
- In the Mass, the one sacrifice of Christ is made present sacramentally.

**Key take-home messages**
- Nothing can separate us from the love of Christ.
- Response is not “try harder,” but trust and surrender.
- Eucharistic faith grows through reverence, curiosity, and prayer.

**Key questions we asked**
- What obstacle currently makes me feel far from God?
- If Jesus truly did this for me, what do I want to say to Him?
- What would change if I believed I am still loved in my weakness?
- At Mass, what do “Do this in memory of me” and “This is my Body” mean?

**Prayers and music used**
- Opening prayer: “Almighty and Eternal Father…”
- Prayer of the heart (“Lord, fill my mind/body/heart/life…”)
- Song: **“This Is My Body”**
- Song prayer with crosses: **“Anima Christi / Soul of Christ”**
- Response “yes” prayer (short surrender prayer)

**Homework / home conversation with children**
- Place a crucifix in a visible place at home.
- Use a short breath prayer in difficult moments.
- Read one Gospel/Pauline text together during the week.
- Teach one reverent Communion habit (clear “Amen,” bow, silence).

## Session 6 — Re-Creation & Response (Thu 5 Mar 2026)

**Core theme:** Jesus can make us new; the Church is where new life is learned and lived.

**Main Scriptures used**
- John 2:1–11 (Wedding at Cana)
- Matthew 5:1–12 (Beatitudes)
- John 6:54–57 (Eucharistic life)

**Kerygma focus**
- Re-Creation: grace transforms us from within (not moral self-improvement).
- Response: “Follow me” becomes concrete through daily “small yes” choices.
- Beatitudes present the portrait of Jesus and of the “new man/new woman” in Christ.
- The Church is communion and “hospital”: healed, fed, forgiven, sent.

**Key take-home messages**
- “Do whatever he tells you” is the path of discipleship.
- New life is possible through grace, sacraments, and community.
- Parents are invited to continue the journey after the programme.

**Key questions we asked**
- Where has the “wine run out” in my family life?
- What am I trying to control instead of giving to Jesus?
- Where is this Beatitude hardest for me (home/work/online)?
- What is one concrete “small yes” I will make this month?

**Prayers and music used**
- Candle invocation and silence
- Full Cana Gospel proclamation
- Beatitudes proclaimed during catechesis
- John 6 proclamation and full **Suscipe** prayer
- Music options: **“Veni Sancte Spiritus”**, **“Be Still”** / **“As the Deer”**, **“Take, Lord, Receive”**, **“Christ Be Our Light”**

**Homework / home conversation with children**
- Choose one Beatitude and practise one concrete family action for a week.
- Bring your “small yes” to Sunday Mass as an offering.
- Come to **Saturday Adoration (9:30–10:30 AM)** when possible.
- Complete the follow-on form and choose one way to keep walking with Jesus in parish life.
